Journey of Uncertainty: Navigating Life's Uncharted Path

Childhood Years

My name is Kurt David V. Tancio, and I’m 20 years old, turning 21 this August 20.1 I was born in Manila on that date in 2004, but my hometown is San Mateo, Rizal, where I grew up for 20 years.2 We moved to different houses within San Mateo, but it always felt like home.3 My childhood memories in San Mateo are some of my best, especially since I witnessed the transformation of the area from a province to a city.4 Living in the province had its charm, but things changed as it grew into a bustling urban environment.5 As a child, I was always immersed in gaming, especially FPS and MOBA games like Counter-Strike, Dota, and Battle Realms.6 I can still vividly remember the excitement of having my first computer set up for gaming.7 My mother would always scold me when I played for too long, but I couldn’t help myself.8 Despite the scoldings, my mother and I have a very close relationship.9 I always tell her about my day, whether it was good or bad, and I’m never shy around her.10 On the other hand, my relationship with my father is more distant.11 We don’t talk much, except when I need to ask for my allowance.12 It’s not that I don’t love him, but we’re simply not as close as I am with my mother.13 I attended Eastern Star Academy for preschool and grade school, where I learned valuable lessons about friendship and decision-making.14 Growing up, I didn’t have many friends since I was an introvert, mostly focused on my games.15 But that didn’t bother me because the games helped me develop important skills like hand-eye coordination, logic, and problem-solving.16 Even now, when I face challenges, I often apply the logic and decision-making skills I honed while gaming.17 Looking back, I see how my gaming experiences shaped who I am today, especially in how I approach problem-solving.18

Teenage Years

My teenage years were some of the best experiences of my life.1 From Grade 7 to 10, I wasn’t an honor student—I was more of an average student.2 In Grade 7, I was just enjoying my life as a first-time high school student, so my grades were around 80-85.3 In Grade 7, I developed an interest in playing basketball, volleyball, and chess.4 Thankfully, I made it to our high school basketball team, and from there, I enjoyed playing basketball more than studying.5 From Grade 7 to 9, I played basketball, but in the middle of Grade 8, I got cut from my basketball team because my grades were failing.6 I needed to improve my grades to return to the team.7 Getting cut from the team humbled me because, even though I was performing very well and winning some awards, they still cut me.8 It was a valuable lesson for me.9 I learned how to manage my time and prioritize what’s important.10 During my varsity years, the school gave us an allowance to spend on important things like maintenance for our bodies, face, etc.11 But as a kid, I ended up spending it on less important things like video game skins and going out with friends.12 I eventually learned how to control it.13 When I got back on the team, I felt like a new person because I had learned my lesson.14 The principal and my mother were very close, and they both scolded me for about two quarters.15 But I'm very thankful for them because it changed my perspective on life.16 Then Grade 10 came, and everything was going fine.17 I was graduating from high school, and then all of a sudden, my mother got sick, and COVID happened.18 This chapter in my life was the most challenging part.19 My mother had cancer, and because of COVID, my siblings and I couldn’t visit the hospital.20 We couldn’t even show her how much we loved her or support her in person.21 We always did video calls, but it wasn’t enough.22 Of course, as her children, we wanted to see her in person, not just on a screen.23 We wanted to take care of her.24 Fast forward through a couple of difficult months—about two to three months.25 In the first month, my mother came home.26 The doctor said she could stay home, and since hospital expenses were extremely high, we decided to bring her home before the bills piled up.27 When she came home, we all cried every day, especially me.28 I'm a mama’s boy, and I love her so much.29 Seeing her lying in bed with a machine attached to her body so she could eat, breathe, drink, and pee broke my heart.30 Whenever I imagine or dream of it, sometimes I cry because it was the most painful phase of my life.31 In the second month, she started experiencing hair loss, which is normal for cancer patients.32 Then came the blood vomiting and other complications.33 In the third month, my mother passed away.34 My heart was shattered into pieces.35 I didn’t know how to act, what to feel, or what to say.36 She was my best friend and my mother.37 Fast forward, she was buried, and I started losing interest in basketball and video games.38 But then I started recovering because I realized that I wasn’t the only one grieving.39 My family was also grieving, and that motivated me to study and return to my hobbies.40 At this time, I was a Grade 11 student.41 I graduated online from my previous school and then studied at Arellano University for two years.42 I chose the ICT strand.43 I only chose it because my father is also in IT, and I wanted to give it a try.44 Then it piqued my interest because it’s very tech-savvy, and the world is advancing, so I thought, why not try it?45 Two years passed, and I graduated from senior high school at Arellano University.46 I also met a very pretty and cute woman from Philippine Women’s University.47 We met in a UV Express, a public transport service.48 Her friend approached me when I was about to get off and asked for my name and socials.49 At first, I was hesitant to message her back because she had messaged me first.50 Then I realized she looked familiar because she was also from San Mateo, and we were both athletes.51 I checked her profile, then we talked, dated, and worked out together.52 We had many similar interests.53 The little things like that made me happy.54 Now, here we are both in our second year of college, pursuing the courses we chose.55 I wish and pray that she becomes very successful.56 I want to give her everything she wants.57 A little backstory—our childhoods were quite different.58 I was spoiled, while she didn't always get what she wanted.59 So whenever we go out, I always make sure to treat her and give her what she wants.60 I really hope she is the one I will marry in the future.61 I guess this is what love does to people.62

College Years

College life so far—my experience at the University of the East has been a little tough.1 Back in Arellano University, when I chose the ICT strand, they didn’t have the lab or equipment to teach us properly.2 I don’t know if the problem was the teacher or the equipment, but for the whole two years, I didn’t learn much.3 I only learned the basics.4 That’s why I’m having a tough time in UE right now.5 But I have friends in UE who are always willing to help whenever I don’t understand the instructions or what to do.6 They are always there, and I’m very thankful for them.7 I wish and pray that my friends and I graduate together, even though we are struggling.8 I also hope to learn the things I need to be a successful IT professional in the near future.9 I’m pursuing this for myself and my family, especially for my mother.10 I want to make her proud.11 I want to learn everything about IT, whatever it takes.12
